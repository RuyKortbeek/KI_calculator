Kovats Retention index (KI)

The Kovats Retention Index (KI) is a measure for the retention of a compound on a chromatography column. This can be calculated using the formula proposed by Kovats (1965) and is based on the retention times of a reference compounds. Here, we use a series of alkanes as a reference.

  • Pz number of carbons of the alkane before RT(x)

Arithmetic Index (AI)

The Arithmetic Index (AI) was proposed by Van den Dool and Kratz (1963) as a more suitable measure when the chromatography column undergoes temperature changes. When using CG-MS with an increasing column temperature ('ramping') it's advised to use the AI instead of the KI.
